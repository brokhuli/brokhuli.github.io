I'm Stephen Ullom — a software architect who has spent the last twenty years pulling order out of complex, physical systems. Trains, reactors, robots, surgical hardware: the kind of software where determinism, observability, and the gap between simulation and reality are not abstract concerns.

I work at the seam between domain experts and code. I lead architecture, mentor engineers, write RFCs, and ship modular monoliths that earn the right to become distributed. I am also a heavy user of AI-augmented development workflows, treating LLMs as engineering teammates with sharp edges rather than magic boxes.

This site is itself a small showcase: spec-driven, statically rendered, zero-JS by default, with a few easter eggs that hint at the kind of systems I love to build.
